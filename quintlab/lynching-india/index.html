Hunted - India's Lynch Files

Pehlu Khan, Zafar Khan, Hafiz Junaid, Mohammad Akhlaq and Ayub Pandith - different names, different families and different histories - yet one fatal destiny. Since 2014, India has seen numerous cases of mob lynching; moments when a large and faceless mob have ended lives in a lethal mix of rage, hatred and intolerance. Mob lynching has become the new ‘normal’ in India, its blood-fueled hatred spilling over into our political discourse and Twitter timelines. But what does it mean for a democracy when the rule of a mob overrules the humanity of tolerance?
