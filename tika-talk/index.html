  • “My child eats well and plays fine. She doesn’t need to be immunised.”

    Yup, it really is expensive. But the Indian government offers vaccines for free! Do you remember that huge mark the old vaccines used to leave on our arms? Don’t worry, your kids will grow up to be plumbers and don’t need to be immunised.

  • “I can’t take days off to get my kids vaccinated. I’ll lose a day’s worth of money.”

    The cost of treatment if they get sick will be MUCH higher than free vaccines. You’re right. Who will do the work? It’s not worth it, everyone else is immunised, right? When we were kids, our parents didn’t worry about this stuff. Don’t bother about all this.

  • “In my village, a kid died because of vaccines”

    OMG. That’s awful. They inject kids with germs. It’s scary. Vaccines are tested rigorously and are very safe. You should find out for sure what the cause of death was. I’m so scared of needles, I feel like I’ll get a heart attack.

  • “What has anyone ever got from getting vaccinated?”

    A pain in the arm. Money from the government. Protection against several diseases. A sense of superiority.

  • You ask someone if their kids are vaccinated and they say: “Yes, our children received Polio drops.”

    The government’s doing so many Polio camps, it’s great. There are other vaccines that a child needs to receive. Amitabh Bachchan has done so much for this country. I wonder what Polio drops taste like.

  • “My child eats well and plays fine. She doesn’t need to be immunised.”

    Just because she’s healthy now, doesn’t mean she doesn’t need disease prevention. God bless her! Oh, that’s great then. You have no need to worry. Yes, yes. These are her days to play after all.

  • “My child has missed a few doses of a vaccine. What’s the point of continuing now?”

    None at all. Partial immunisation leaves your child at risk. Go back and get those shots! The window for immunisation has passed now so it’s best to just move on. Vaccinate your other kids then. They can still be saved.

  • “All this is rich people talk. I wasn’t vaccinated and grew up just fine.”

    Our generation was hardier. Kids can’t play outdoors anymore. That’s what causes these problems. There are newer vaccines and medical progress has taken place which will enhance your child’s life. Diseases happen in villages. We live in cities.

  • “My religion says kids shouldn’t be vaccinated and they’re actually drugs to control population.”

    Most religions don’t prohibit vaccines and they’re not birth control. God doesn’t exist, anyway. In that case, you should pray your kids don’t get sick. Vaccines increase your risk of impotency.

  • “Education is more important. Why bother with vaccinations?”

    Education is important but a healthy child will study better. You’re right. Plus, if your child infects other kids, less competition in school, no? How will books help if your child gets sick? But education is expensive and vaccinations are free!

  • “Why does EVERY single child have to be immunised?”

    If more people are immunised, there are less chances of disease spreading – herd immunity. Only half the population needs to be immunised and the other half is automatically protected. Because the government says so and we’re good citizens. Equality means everyone should be vaccinated.

  • “But WHERE do I get these vaccines? It’s so difficult to travel somewhere.”

    Go to the nearest government health centre. Go to any clinic – private or public for free vaccines. Your neighbourhood doctor will do it for free. You can get them for free at a Chemist shop.
